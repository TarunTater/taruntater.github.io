EMNLP 2025 2025 ★ SAC Highlight Award
AbsVis – Benchmarking How Humans and Vision-Language Models "See" Abstract Concepts in Images
Tater, T. et al.
We introduce AbsVis, the first large-scale dataset of 10k+ human-annotated explanations for abstract concepts in images, enabling systematic comparison between human reasoning and Vision–Language Models (VLMs) such as LLaVA and Qwen. Models fine-tuned via DPO on human annotations show improved alignment with subjective visual reasoning.

AbsVis — Architecture Overview (EMNLP 2025)

AbsVis collects human explanations for abstract concepts (justice, freedom, love…) found in images, then benchmarks both baseline VLMs and DPO-fine-tuned VLMs against these annotations. A VLM-as-Judge approach scores alignment between model and human reasoning.

Systematic comparison of visual attribute spaces for concrete vs. abstract concepts. Analyzes variability in how images represent a concept, nearest-neighbor structure in embedding space, and identifies which conceptual categories are hardest for current models.

Modular Unsupervised Sarcasm Generation (EMNLP 2019)

A modular pipeline for generating sarcasm without parallel training data. An Information Retrieval module fetches sarcasm priors; a Seq2Seq encoder generates candidate outputs; a reward-based RL-LSTM steers generation toward ironic, contextually appropriate sarcasm.
